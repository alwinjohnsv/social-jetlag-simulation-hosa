Understand Your Social Jetlag

Social jetlag is the mismatch between your biological clock and your social obligations— like waking up early for school or work on weekdays and sleeping in on weekends.

This site lets you explore how your sleep schedule, habits, and biology interact over time. Use the simulations to visualize your sleep patterns, experiment with changes, and see how social jetlag can affect your body.

Quick Facts

  • Definition: Difference between mid-sleep on workdays vs. free days.
  • Common: Especially in teens and young adults with early school/work times.
  • Linked to: Fatigue, mood changes, metabolic risk, and lower performance.

Why Social Jetlag Matters

Chronic Misalignment

Even a 1–2 hour shift between weekday and weekend sleep can create ongoing circadian misalignment.

Performance & Mood

Irregular sleep is associated with lower attention, slower reaction times, and more irritability.

Long-Term Health

Persistent social jetlag is linked in research to higher risk of metabolic and cardiovascular issues.
